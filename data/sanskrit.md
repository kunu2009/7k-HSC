This is a comprehensive, chapter-wise guide to the Sanskrit Alhad (संस्कृत आल्हाद) textbook, structured to maximize your preparation for the Maharashtra Class 12 HSC board exam.

The textbook is generally divided into five main sections (*Maṇḍalas*), followed by core grammar and composition requirements.

---

## I. प्रथमं मंडलम्: सुगम संस्कृतम् (Functional Sanskrit)
**(Exam Weightage: 6 Marks)**

This section tests your practical usage and classification skills. It is known for providing easy, high-scoring marks if practiced correctly.

### Chapter 1: व्यावहारिक सूचना (Vyavahārika Sūchanā)
*   **Content:** Sentences or instructions related to real-world situations, often presented alongside pictures.
*   **Exam Task (3 Marks):** You will be given three images/situations and must write **Sanskrit sentences or instructions** describing them.
*   **Study Focus:** You must practice writing accurate, complete Sanskrit sentences for various common activities (e.g., 'Do this', 'Go there', 'See this'). You are expected to write only the instructions/sentences, not to draw the pictures.

### Chapter 2: व्यावहारिक शब्द स्तम्भकः (Vyavahārika Śabda Stambhakaḥ)
*   **Content:** Vocabulary focused on specific thematic areas.
*   **Exam Task (3 Marks):** You are given a list of words (e.g., 8 words) and must **classify them into columns (*Stambhakāḥ*)** with headings like नाट्यम् (Drama), वाद्यम् (Instrument/Music), or कला (Art).
*   **Study Focus:** **Memorize the vocabulary lists** provided in the textbook or study guides that categorize words for common themes, such as:
    *   **नाट्यम् (Drama):** Words like नटी (actress), भाव (emotion), or अभिनय (acting).
    *   **वाद्यम् (Instruments):** Words like वीणा (Vina), जलतरंगम् (Jalatarangam).
    *   **Study Tip:** Each correct word classified is typically worth half a mark, totaling 3 marks.

---

## II. द्वितीयं मंडलम्: पद्यम् (Poetry Section)
**(Exam Weightage: 12 Marks)**

This section involves reading and understanding two poetry passages (*Padyānśas*).

### General Exam Tasks for Poetry (4 Marks per passage):

1.  **अवबोधनम् (Comprehension):** Answering questions based on the passage.
    *   Answer in full sentences (*Pūrṇa Vākyena Uttarata*).
    *   Identifying the correct reason (*Uchitam Kāranam*), or True/False (*Satya/Asatya*).
    *   Filling the blank in a box/square (*Uchitam Śabda Catuṣkoṇam Pūrayata*).
2.  **शब्दज्ञानम् (Vocabulary/Grammar):** Includes finding antonyms (*Viruddhārthak Śabda*), finding *Viśeṣaṇa-Viśeṣya* pairs, or performing *Sandhi* splits.
3.  **Critical Task (4 Marks, Option-based):** You choose one of the following:
    *   **अन्वय पूर्ती (Anvaya Pūrtiḥ):** Completing the prose order of the verse using words from a box.
    *   **सरलार्थ (Saralārtha):** Writing the simple meaning/translation of the verse into your medium language (English or Marathi).

### Key Poetry Chapters:

#### 1. क्षणं मनोविनोदनम् (Kshaṇam Manovinodanam)
*   **Theme:** Collection of beautiful sayings (*Subhāṣitas*) intended for momentary entertainment and reflection.

#### 2. उत्सवप्रिया देवभूमिः (Utsavapriyā Devabhūmiḥ)
*   **Source:** An excerpt from *Śrī Devadeveśvara Mahākāvyam*.
*   **Theme:** Festivals and the sacred land (India). It is based on a specific epic poem, so be prepared to identify the name of the source if asked.

#### 3. भावेन भावः क्रियया क्रिया (Bhāvena Bhāvaḥ Kriyayā Kriyā)
*   **Theme/Concept:** This chapter contains verses focused heavily on ethics, conduct, practical wisdom, and the importance of timely action.
*   **Key Concept Example:** The verse emphasizes that learning (*Vidyā*) written only in a book (*Pustakasthā Tu Yā Vidyā*) is useless if it cannot be deployed at the required time (*Kāryakāle Samutpanne*). Similarly, money entrusted to others (*Parahastagataṃ Dhanam*) is not yours when needed.
*   **Key Concept Example (Health):** It contains wisdom regarding health, noting that if a person follows a proper diet (*Pathya*), medicine (*Auṣadhi*) is not needed; if they ignore the proper diet, medicine is useless.

## III. तृतीयं मंडलम्: गद्यम् (Prose Section)
**(Exam Weightage: 9 Marks)**

The syllabus contains three main prose chapters (*Gadyam*), from which passages and key contextual questions are drawn.

### Key Prose Chapters:

1.  **भाषा संवादः (Bhāṣā Saṃvādaḥ)**
    *   **Theme:** A dialogue where different languages interact. It highlights **Sanskrit as the common thread (*Samānasūtram*)** among various Indian languages.
    *   **Context:** The chapter uses imaginary conversations to discuss word origins and linguistics, often related to concepts described by figures like Muni (perhaps Bharata Muni, author of *Nāṭyaśāstra*).

2.  **प्राचीन काल प्रसाधनानि (Prāchīna Kāla Prasādhanāni)**
    *   **Theme:** Discusses embellishments or practices (*Prasādhanāni*) prevalent in ancient times.

3.  **प्रजापालनार्थं राजकर्तव्यानि (Prajāpālanārtha Rājā Kartavyāni)**
    *   **Theme:** The duties of a king for the proper protection and maintenance of the citizens (*Prajāpālana*). This text may derive from classical texts like the *Arthaśāstra*.

### Exam Focus for Prose:

*   **Comprehension:** Similar to poetry, direct questions are asked, including *Prashna Nirmāṇam* (Question Construction).
*   **Identifying Source (1 Mark):** You may be asked the **name of the lesson** (*Pāṭhasya Nāma*) and the **original source text/book name** (*Granthasya Nāma*) from which the passage is taken.
*   **माध्यम भाषायाः ससंदर्भम् स्पष्टीकरणम् (Mādhyama Bhāṣā)**: This is a critical, high-scoring question (typically 4–5 marks).
    *   **Requirement:** Answer in detail **in your medium language** (English/Marathi) based on the context of the chapter themes.
    *   **Strategy:** You must follow a clear structure: **Introduction, Main Content/Argument, and Conclusion**. Write a minimum of **10 to 12 lines**. These answers are based on thematic questions from the three core prose chapters or the drama section, and **the passage itself is generally not provided**.

## IV. चतुर्थं मंडलम्: साहित्य परिचय / साहित्य विशेषः च (Literature and Culture)
**(Exam Weightage: 6 Marks)**

This section is strictly knowledge-based, relying on objective or short answers without a source passage.

*   **Key Topics:**
    1.  **भारत विद्या (Bhārata Vidyā):** Introduction to Indian knowledge systems.
    2.  **नाट्य विशेषः (Nāṭya Viśeṣaḥ):** Special aspects of drama/theatre.
    3.  **General Information:** Knowledge of the **Vedas, Vedangas, and texts on various Sciences**.
*   **Study Focus:** You must read this content multiple times, focusing on names, definitions, and specific facts, as you will be asked to identify the correct option (*Uchita Vikalpa*). Review this section just before the exam, as recall is critical.

## V. पंचमं मंडलम्: नाट्यकलशः (Drama Section)
**(Exam Weightage: 8 Marks)**

This section features excerpts from prescribed dramas.

*   **Key Excerpts (Nāṭya Praveśa):** The dramatic scenes include *Bhīmaghaṭotkacayoḥ* (Bhima and Ghatotkacha), *Duṣyanta Sarvadamanyoḥ Putra Saṅgamo* (Dushyanta and Sarvadaman meeting his son), and *Putra Saṅgamo* (Meeting of father and son).
*   **Exam Task:** A drama passage (*Nāṭyānśa*) is given, followed by comprehension (*Avabodhanam*) and grammatical analysis/segregation (*Pṛthakkaraṇam*).
*   **Preparation Tip:** Although the passage is provided for direct questions, you should know the basic plot and context of each dramatic piece, especially to handle potential *Mādhyama Bhāṣā* questions related to the drama.

## VI. व्याकरणम् (Vyākaraṇam / Grammar)
**(Exam Weightage: 12 Marks)**

Grammar tests knowledge acquired up to Standard XI. This is a compulsory section:

1.  **नामतालिका पूरयत (Nominal Forms):** Completing tables for nominal forms (nouns, pronouns, adjectives) based on gender, case (*Vibhakti*), or number (*Vacanam*).
    *   *Syllabus includes:* *Svarānta* (vowel-ending) and *Vyañjanānta* (consonant-ending) words, as well as pronouns (*Sarvanāmam*).
2.  **समासतालिका पूरयत (Compounds):** Identifying, dissolving (*Vigraha*), or naming the compound (*Samāsa*) type.
    *   *Syllabus includes:* अव्ययीभाव (Avyayibhāva), तत्पुरुष (Tatpuruṣa), द्वन्द्व (Dvandva), and बहुव्रीहि (Bahuvrīhi).
3.  **कृदन्त-तद्धितान्त चित्वा लिखत (Derivatives):** Classifying words as verb derivatives (*Kṛdanta*) or noun derivatives (*Taddhitānta*).
4.  **सूचनानुसारं परिवर्तनं कुरुत (Transformations):** Performing grammatical changes as instructed. (You usually solve 3 out of 5 options).
    *   **Key Transformation Topics:**
        *   **Voice Change (*Vācya Parivartana*):** Converting between active (*Kartari*), passive (*Karmaṇi*), and impersonal (*Bhāva Prayoga*) voices.
        *   **Tenses and Moods (*Kriyāpada Rūpāṇi*):** Changing verb forms (e.g., from Present to Imperative or Future) for 1st and 2nd *Gaṇas* (conjugations) in *Parasmaipada* and *Ātmanepada*.
        *   **Participles:** Using or removing *Satī Saptamī* (Locative Absolute) or indeclinable participles.

## VII. लेखन कौशलम् (Composition Skills)
**(Exam Weightage: 16 Marks)**

This section is vital for securing full marks and requires practiced writing:

1.  **निबंध लेखनम् (Essay Writing):** Write a descriptive essay (e.g., *Nibandha*) of approximately **10 sentences** in Sanskrit. Essays are usually worth 8 marks.
    *   **Tip:** Although not compulsory, using a *Subhāṣita* (Sanskrit verse) is often seen as adding quality, but ensure it is accurate, as an incorrect one will lead to loss of marks.

2.  **चित्रपट वर्णनम् (Picture Description):** Complete a paragraph about a picture by filling eight blanks using words provided in a box (*Mañjūṣā*).
    *   **Crucial Rule:** You **must write the entire paragraph** and **underline the filled words**.

3.  **Translation and Story Completion:** You must also be prepared for:
    *   Translation of **ten sentences into Sanskrit**.
    *   Completing a **gap story** (about 20 gaps).

4.  **वृत्तान्त लेखनम् (Report Writing):** Writing a report in Sanskrit based on provided input.

---

## VIII. अष्टमः विभागः – अपठितम् (Unseen Passage / Translation)

1.  **Unseen Passage:** Answer questions on a given unseen Sanskrit passage in the medium offered.
2.  **Translation (4 Marks):** Translate several Sanskrit sentences into your medium language. You must translate **4 out of 6** sentences.

### Final Study Analogy

To achieve success in the Sanskrit board exam, view your study material like crafting a fine temple: the **Prose and Poetry passages (Gadyam and Padyam)** form the foundation and structure; the **Grammar (Vyākaraṇam)** acts as the specialized tools and strong mortar holding every piece together; and the **Composition (Lekhana Kauśalyam)** is the intricate carvings and finishing touches that showcase true mastery. Each part must be prepared precisely to complete the whole.This comprehensive guide will detail the next three key chapters/sections in the Sanskrit Alhad textbook, starting with the remainder of the Poetry section (*Dvitīya Maṇdalam*) and moving into the first Prose chapter (*Tṛtīya Maṇdalam*).

**(Total Exam Weightage: 12 Marks)**

We continue with the poetry lessons, which are crucial for comprehension (*Avabodhanam*), translation (*Saralārtha*), and metrical prose order (*Anvaya Pūrtiḥ*).

***

### Chapter 3: मोदं वितनोतु संवादः (Modaṃ Vitanotu Saṃvādaḥ)

*   **Meaning:** May the Dialogue Spread Joy.
*   **Theme and Nature:** This section consists of beautiful sayings (*Subhāṣitas*) presented in a **dialogue format (*Saṃvādatmakāni Subhāṣitāni*)**. They are often witty, employing subtle humour and sometimes *Śleṣa* (double meaning), providing entertainment (*Vinoda*). The dialogues occur between various characters, including deities, humans, animals, and birds.
*   **Key Concepts and Verses:**

    1.  **Question of Identity (Krishna/Beggar):** A dialogue involves a beggar who, upon seeing Krishna, offers obeisance (*namo namaḥ*) not to Krishna, but to Daridra (poverty), as poverty keeps him attached to the world.
    2.  **Shiva-Parvati Dialogue (*Ambikā*):** This famous dialogue relies heavily on *Śleṣa* (punning) where Shiva uses names that have dual meanings (e.g., *Nīlakaṇṭha* meaning "blue-throated" for Shiva, but also "blue jay/peacock" for Parvati).
        *   When Parvati asks who he is, Shiva says: "I am *Nīlakaṇṭha*" (Shiva). Parvati replies: "If you are a *Nīlakaṇṭha* (peacock), then why are you here? Go to the forest where they live.".
        *   This conversational style, using ambiguity to create humour, demonstrates literary skill.
    3.  **Kārttikeya (Ṣaṇmukha) and the Ocean (*Sāgara*):** The dialogue involves Kārttikeya asking the ocean to identify another man who would willingly bring shame upon his head. The ocean replies that only Lord Shiva, who holds the moon on his head, is such a person, demonstrating the witty use of context.
*   **Exam Focus:** Ensure you understand the underlying pun in the Shiva-Parvati dialogue and the contextual meaning of key terms like *Nīlakaṇṭha* and *Paśupati* (Lord of the Beasts/Shiva).

***

### Chapter 4: भावेन भावः क्रियया क्रिया (Bhāvena Bhāvaḥ Kriyayā Kriyā)

*   **Meaning:** Action by Feeling, Action by Action.
*   **Theme:** This section compiles verses focusing on **ethics, wise conduct, and morality** (नैतिक सुभाषितानि).
*   **Key Concepts and Verses:**

    1.  **Internal Contentment:** The verse stresses that the world reflects your inner state: **first seek to please your own mind** (*Yādṛśaṃ prasannam*) to see the world as pleasant.
    2.  **Useless Knowledge/Wealth:** The famous saying: **Knowledge that is merely in a book (*Pustakasthā Tu Yā Vidyā*) or wealth that is in another person’s hands (*Parahastagataṃ Dhanam*) is not useful at the time of need (*Kāryakāle Samutpanne*)**. This highlights the need for applicable knowledge and accessible wealth.
    3.  **The Importance of Diet (*Pathya*):** A verse advises that if a sick person follows a proper diet (*Pathya*), medicine (*Auṣadhi*) is not needed; if they do not follow the proper diet, medicine is useless.
    4.  **Foreseeing Adversity:** The wise should ponder the counter-action (*pratikriyā*) before the adversity (*Vipadām ādau*) actually occurs, just as it is futile to dig a well when the house is already burning (*pradīpte na gṛhe kūpakhananaṃ yuktam*).
    5.  **The Bee and the Lotus (Determination):** A poignant verse where a bee, trapped inside a lotus at night, contemplates its release at dawn but is killed by an elephant crushing the lotus. This is often used to illustrate the philosophical concept: **"I shall either achieve my goal (*Kāryam vā sādhyeyaṃ*) or sacrifice my body (*dehaṃ vā pātyeyam*)**.

*   **Exam Focus:** These philosophical verses are excellent candidates for the **Simple Meaning/Translation (*Saralārtha*)** question (4 marks). Additionally, the ethical advice (e.g., on health or knowledge) may be asked in the **Medium Language Answer** section.

***

## III. तृतीयं मंडलम्: गद्यम् (Prose Section)
**(Total Exam Weightage: 9 Marks)**

This section begins the three compulsory prose chapters. Prose passages are the source of comprehension, vocabulary/grammar, and the structured medium language answer (*Mādhyama Bhāṣā*).

### Chapter 1: भाषा संवादः (Bhāṣā Saṃvādaḥ)

*   **Meaning:** Dialogue of Languages.
*   **Theme:** This text is a **fictional dialogue** between various Indian languages, where they discuss their common origin and connection to **Sanskrit**. It emphasizes that Sanskrit is the **common thread (*Samānasūtram*)** among Indian languages.
*   **Content and Context (Linguistics):**
    *   The chapter delves into the ancient science of word origin and derivation called **Nirukta**, referencing the work of Yāska and Pāṇini.
    *   The discussion highlights how new words are created in languages through **five elements (*Pañcatattva*)** of *Nirvacanam* (Explanation/Derivation):
        1.  **Varṇa-Brahma:** Acquiring a new letter/sound. (E.g., Sanskrit word acquiring a new sound in other languages)
        2.  **Varṇa-Viparyaya:** Reversal or rearrangement of letters. (E.g., *Saptāha* becoming *Haptā* in some contexts)
        3.  **Varṇa-Vikāra:** Change in the letter/sound.
        4.  **Varṇa-Nāśa:** Loss or deletion of a letter/sound.
        5.  **Dhātu-Yoga:** Association with a root/base verb (*Dhātu*). (E.g., the root *chal* meaning 'to move' leading to various words like *chala, chalaḥ* etc.)
    *   Saraswati, the Goddess of Speech, explains that while Nirukta seeks the true meaning of words, the modern interpretation of the Sanskrit term **"artha" (meaning)** is the soul of the word.

*   **Exam Focus:**
    *   **Passage-Based Questions:** Expect comprehension (answering in full sentences) and identification of the derivation principles.
    *   **Vocabulary/Grammar:** Look for *Viśeṣaṇa-Viśeṣya* pairs (adjective-noun matches) and *Nāmasthāne Sarvanāmam* (replacing a noun with a pronoun).
    *   **Medium Language Answer (Mādhyama Bhāṣā):** This chapter is a frequent source for the 4-mark medium language question (प्र. 3 (ब)). Focus on the **role of Sanskrit as the unifying force** and the **explanation of the five elements of *Nirvacanam***. You must write a structured answer (Introduction, Argument, Conclusion) of 10-12 lines.

## IV. चतुर्थं मंडलम्: साहित्य परिचय / साहित्य विशेषः च (Literature and Culture)
**(Total Exam Weightage: 6 Marks)**

This section is unique as it is based purely on **factual knowledge** and general information about Indian culture and literature, rather than specific passages.

### 1. भारत विद्या (Bhārata Vidyā)

*   **Theme:** Introduction to Indian Knowledge Systems, history, and culture.
*   **Key Areas of Study:**
    *   **Scope:** Covers the ancient history, social, economic, religious, and political aspects of ancient India.
    *   **Sources of History:** Focus on literary works, inscriptional evidence (like *Dāna Patra*—gift inscriptions—and *Rāja Praśasti*—royal eulogies), and archaeological findings.
    *   **Ancient Scripts:** Awareness of ancient scripts like Brāhmī, Kharōṣṭī, Śāradā, and Granthalipi.
    *   **Ancient Arts and Sciences:** Includes architecture (*Vāstu*), sculpture (*Mūrti*), carving (*Saṅkūlana*), and protection/preservation (*Surakṣaṇam*).

### 2. नाट्य विशेषः (Nāṭya Viśeṣaḥ - Dramatic Arts)

*   **Theme:** Special characteristics and technical terms related to Sanskrit drama.
*   **Key Topics:** Definitions of drama types and technical terms. You may be asked about the root of Sanskrit drama, which is sometimes derived from *Kṛtka Nāṭaka* (artificial or derived drama).
*   **Exam Task (प्र. 5):** Six objective questions (*Uchita Vikalpam*—Choose the Correct Alternative) are expected, worth 1 mark each. You **must** revise these facts frequently, as no passage context is provided.

***

## V. पंचमं मंडलम्: नाट्यकलशः (Drama Section)
**(Total Exam Weightage: 8 Marks)**

This section includes specific extracts from Sanskrit dramas.

*   **Key Excerpts (Nāṭyakalaśaḥ):** Passages are drawn from scenes like *Bhīmaghaṭotkacayoḥ* (Bhīma and Ghaṭotkaca), *Duṣyanta Sarvadamanyoḥ Putra Saṅgamo* (Dushyanta and Sarvadaman), and sometimes extracts where characters discuss the purpose of study (*Adhyayan*).
*   **Exam Tasks (प्र. 4):**
    1.  **Comprehension (अवबोधनम्):** Answering direct questions (e.g., *Pūrṇa Vākyena Uttarata*) based on the dramatic passage.
    2.  **Analysis (शब्दज्ञानम् / पृथक्करणम्):** This involves:
        *   **Identifying Cause/Reason:** Choosing the correct reason (*Uchitam Kāranam*) for a character's action.
        *   **Question Construction (*Prashna Nirmāṇam*):** Creating a question based on an underlined word in the passage.
        *   **Finding Indeclinables:** Identifying *Hetvarthak Dhātusādhit Avyay* (meaning 'for the purpose of...').
        *   **Sequencing (*Kramena Yojayitvā*):** Arranging the events of the dialogue or scene in the correct chronological order.

*   **Study Strategy:** Because these passages are given, focus less on memorizing the plot, and more on **perfecting your structural analysis of the text, grammar, and vocabulary** to quickly answer the comprehension and separation tasks. You should still know the background for the *Mādhyama Bhāṣā* question, as the paragraph is not provided for that answer.

## VI. षष्ठः विभागः – लेखन कौशलम् (Composition Skills)
**(Total Exam Weightage: 16 Marks)**

This is where you demonstrate Sanskrit writing proficiency.

### 1. निबंध लेखनम् (Essay Writing)
*   **Task:** Write an essay (*Nibandha*) in Sanskrit on one of the given topics (e.g., *Bhāratavidyā* or *Paropakāraḥ*).
*   **Requirement:** The essay should be between **8 to 10 sentences** (6–10 sentences as per some sources). Focus on accurate grammar and rich vocabulary.

### 2. चित्रपट वर्णनम् (Picture Description)
*   **Task:** Complete a paragraph describing a picture by filling in the blanks using a given word box (*Mañjūṣā*).
*   **Crucial Rule:** You **must rewrite the entire paragraph** and **underline the filled-in words** to show the examiner what you have inserted. If you do not underline the inserted words, marks may be lost.

### 3. वृत्तान्त लेखनम् (Report Writing)
*   **Task:** Write a report (*Vṛttāntalekhanam*) in Sanskrit based on the information given in an invitation letter (*Nimantrapatra*) or other supplied data.

## VII. सप्तमः विभागः – भाषाभ्यासः (Grammar Practice)
**(Total Exam Weightage: 12 Marks)**

The final compulsory sections are dedicated to grammar, where accuracy is paramount.

*   **नामतालिका पूरयत (Nominal Forms):** Complete tables based on the correct gender (*Liṅgam*), case (*Vibhakti*), and number (*Vacanam*) for nouns, pronouns, and adjectives, including *Svarānta* (vowel-ending) and *Vyañjanānta* (consonant-ending) words, and pronouns (*Sarvanāmam*).
*   **समासतालिका पूरयत (Compounds):** Complete tables by identifying the compound name (*Samāsanāmam*), the compound word (*Samastapadam*), or the dissolution (*Vigraha*). The syllabus includes: *Karmadhāraya* (*Stutikanyā*), *Sahabuvrīhi* (*Anilena Saha*), *Nitya Avyayībhāva* (*Pratidinam*), *Itaretara Dvandva* (*Strīpuruṣau*), *Nañ Tatpuruṣa* (*Na Kṛpa*), *Tṛtīyā Tatpuruṣa* (*Artha Hīnaḥ*), and *Prādi Tatpuruṣa* (*Prakṛtyā Sthitaḥ*).
*   **कृदन्त-तद्धितान्त चित्वा लिखत (Derivatives):** Classify given words into *Kṛdanta* (verb derivatives) and *Taddhitānta* (noun derivatives).
*   **सूचनानुसारं परिवर्तनं कुरुत (Transformations):** Solve three out of five transformations.
    *   **Vācya Parivartanam** (Voice Change): Converting between *Kartari* (Active) and *Karmaṇi* (Passive).
    *   **Lakaar Parivartanam** (Tense Change): Converting forms (e.g., to Past Tense (*Laṅ Lakaar*)).
    *   **Participle Replacement:** Using *Pūrvakālavācaka Dhātusādhit Avyay* (Indeclinable Participles).
    *   **Satī Saptamī:** Converting a clause to or removing *Satī Saptamī* (Locative Absolute).
*   **Synonyms (समानार्थक शब्द):** Finding synonyms for words (e.g., *Agni, Mārjāra, Kuntala*).

### 1. चित्रपट वर्णनम् (Picture Description)

*   **Weightage:** 8 Marks.
*   **Task:** Complete a descriptive paragraph (e.g., eight blanks) using words from the **word box (*Mañjūṣā*)**.
*   **Mandatory Rule:** You **must rewrite the entire paragraph** and **underline the word(s) inserted** in the blanks. Failure to underline may lead to mark deduction.

### 2. निबंध लेखनम् (Essay Writing)

*   **Weightage:** 8 Marks.
*   **Task:** Write a short descriptive essay in Sanskrit on one of the given topics (e.g., *Bhāratavidyā*, *Paropakāraḥ*, *Mama Priya Abhineta*).
*   **Requirement:** The length should be concise, typically **6 to 10 sentences**. Although optional, adding an accurate *Śloka* (verse) can improve the essay's quality, but an incorrect verse will penalize your score.

### 3. वृत्तान्त लेखनम् (Report Writing)

*   **Weightage:** 7 Marks (or 8 Marks).
*   **Task:** Convert information, often from an **invitation letter (*Nimantraṇapatrikā*)**, into a continuous Sanskrit report (*Vṛttānta*).
